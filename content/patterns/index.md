You usually meet a pattern in the middle of a problem. A handler keeps growing. A queue backs up. A service starts reaching into places it should not. You make a small choice, then another, and the shape of the code begins to change.

Patterns help you name that shape. They do not tell you what to do in every case. They give you a way to compare options, see the cost early, and pick the tradeoff you can carry.

This catalog is grouped into six families, **Creational**, **Structural**, **Behavioral**, **Concurrency**, **Synchronisation**, and **Architectural**. Each page stays close to implementation, so you can see where a pattern fits, where it drags, and what tends to break when used too early.

Use the sidebar. Move by family when you are scanning, or jump straight to a pattern when you already know the pressure you are under.
