---
title: Design Philosophy
intro: Software architecture isn't a set of rules to follow. It's a way of thinking about code.
---

Patterns are implementations. Principles are the reason implementations work.

The gap between messy code and well-designed systems often comes down to not knowing the questions to ask. The principles here — SOLID and test-driven thinking — aren't doctrine. They're lenses that change what you see when you look at a design problem.

These ideas aren't new. They come from decades of people wrestling with the same problems: Robert C. Martin, Eric Evans, Martin Fowler, and many others. Ground yourself in this thinking before the pattern catalog, and the patterns become easier to understand — and harder to misapply.
