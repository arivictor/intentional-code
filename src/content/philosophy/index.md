---
title: Design Philosophy
intro: Software architecture isn't a set of rules to follow. It's a way of thinking about code.
---

Patterns are implementations. Principles are the reason implementations work.

The gap between messy code and well-designed systems often comes down to not knowing the questions to ask. The principles here aren't doctrine. They're lenses that change what you see when you look at a design problem — and they interact with each other. SOLID tells you how to structure types. Separation of Concerns tells you how to structure systems. KISS and YAGNI tell you when to stop. Gall's Law tells you why complex systems designed from scratch consistently fail.

These ideas aren't new. They come from decades of people wrestling with the same problems: Robert C. Martin, Eric Evans, Martin Fowler, Ron Jeffries, John Gall, and many others. Ground yourself in this thinking before the pattern catalog, and the patterns become easier to understand — and harder to misapply.
